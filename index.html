About

Born and raised in a small town in Ohio, and not being very musically or athletically inclined, computers and video games were what took up most of my time. I was introduced to video games when I was very young, around 2 or 3, by my dad and older brother. The first game I remember watching them play was Resident Evil 2, and the first I remember playing myself was Mortal Kombat. This sparked an interest in me that continued on, especially when I started school and realized I worked well with computers. When I was in high school, I took a computer programming class where I learned the basics of HTML and CSS. I also took robotics, where we programmed robotic arms to do different things, such as write our names on a piece of paper or build structures out of blocks. These classes are what confirmed in my mind that I wanted to work with computers for the rest of my life. When I read about Neumont College of Computer Science, I knew they offered the classes most neccesary to my goals.

During my time at Neumont, I've majored in Software and Game Development, where I've learned the basics of C++, and even created a game engine from scratch. I've also learned how to use Blender to 3D model.
